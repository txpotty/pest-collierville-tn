Common Pest Problems in Collierville, TN and How We Can Help

Collierville, like many areas, is susceptible to a variety of common pests that can invade homes and businesses. Understanding these pests and knowing the signs of an infestation is the first step in addressing the problem. Collierville Pest Pros has extensive experience dealing with the pests most prevalent in our region, providing effective and lasting solutions.

Ant Infestations

Ants are a ubiquitous pest, and while often seemingly harmless individually, large colonies can become a significant nuisance and even pose health risks depending on the species. They enter structures seeking food, water, or shelter and can quickly establish extensive colonies within walls, floors, and foundations. Seeing trails of ants, especially in kitchens or bathrooms, or finding small piles of sawdust-like material (frass) can indicate an ant problem. Different species of ants require different treatment approaches, which is why professional identification is crucial. Our ant control services involve identifying the species, locating the colony, and implementing targeted treatments to eliminate the entire infestation, not just the visible ants. We also advise on preventative measures to keep these persistent insects from returning.

Termite Damage

Termites are one of the most destructive pests, capable of causing significant structural damage to homes and businesses before their presence is even detected. They feed on cellulose materials, primarily wood, and can silently tunnel through beams, walls, and foundations. Signs of termites can include discarded wings near windowsills or doors, mud tubes on exterior walls, and hollow-sounding wood. Termite infestations require immediate and professional intervention to prevent further damage. Our termite treatment services include thorough inspections, customized treatment plans utilizing advanced methods, and ongoing monitoring to ensure eradication and prevent future infestations. Protecting your property from termite damage is a critical investment.

Bed Bug Problems

Bed bugs are tiny, elusive pests that feed on the blood of humans and animals. They are notorious for causing itchy bites and significant distress. Bed bugs can be found in mattresses, box springs, furniture, and even in cracks and crevices in walls and floors. Signs of bed bugs include small red or brown spots on bedding (fecal matter), shed skins, and of course, bites on the skin (often in a line or cluster). Bed bug infestations are notoriously difficult to eliminate without professional help due to their ability to hide and their resistance to many over-the-counter products. Our bed bug extermination services utilize a combination of treatments, often including heat treatments and targeted insecticide applications, to effectively eliminate bed bugs at all life stages. We understand the urgency and sensitivity of bed bug infestations and provide discreet and efficient service.

Rodent Infestations

Rodents, such as mice and rats, are not only a nuisance but also pose significant health risks and can cause considerable damage to properties. They can chew through wiring, insulation, and structural components and contaminate food and surfaces with their droppings and urine. Signs of a rodent infestation include droppings, gnaw marks, scratching sounds in walls or ceilings, and sightings of the rodents themselves. Rodents reproduce quickly, so a small problem can rapidly escalate into a large infestation. Our rodent control services involve identifying the type of rodent, locating entry points and nesting sites, implementing trapping and baiting strategies, and sealing entry points to prevent future access. We focus on effective and humane methods for rodent removal and prevention.

Spider Sightings

While most spiders in the Collierville area are harmless, their presence can be unsettling for many people, and some species, like the brown recluse and black widow, pose a legitimate health threat. Spiders are often found in quiet, undisturbed areas like attics, basements, garages, and crawl spaces, spinning webs to catch prey. Seeing webs, especially in high-traffic areas, or encountering spiders indoors are common signs of a spider problem. Our spider removal services involve identifying the species, treating areas where spiders are active (like corners, eaves, and entry points), and removing existing webs and egg sacs. We aim to reduce the spider population and make your property less attractive to these eight-legged visitors.

Cockroach Problems

Cockroaches are unsanitary pests that can contaminate food, spread diseases, and trigger allergies and asthma. They are resilient and can thrive in a variety of environments, often found in kitchens, bathrooms, basements, and other damp or dark areas. Signs of a cockroach infestation include seeing live cockroaches (especially at night), finding fecal droppings (which resemble coffee grounds or black pepper), discovering egg cases, and noticing a distinct, musty odor. Cockroaches reproduce rapidly and their ability to hide makes them difficult to eliminate without professional help. Our cockroach control services utilize a combination of baits, traps, and targeted insecticides to eliminate cockroach populations and prevent their return. We also provide advice on sanitation and other preventative measures.

Flea Troubles

Fleas are small, wingless insects that feed on the blood of mammals and birds. They are a common problem for pet owners but can infest homes even if you don’t have pets, hitchhiking on other animals or even people. Flea bites are itchy and can cause discomfort for both humans and pets. Signs of fleas include seeing the fleas themselves (especially on pets), finding flea dirt (fecal matter that looks like black pepper) on bedding or furniture, and noticing your pets scratching more than usual. A significant flea infestation can be challenging to eliminate, requiring treatment of both the pets and the environment. Our flea treatment services involve treating infested areas within the home and providing recommendations for treating your pets (in consultation with your veterinarian). We use effective products and techniques to break the flea life cycle and eliminate the infestation.

Bee and Wasp Nests

Bees and wasps play important roles in our ecosystem, but nests located near homes or high-traffic areas can pose a sting risk to people and pets, especially for those with allergies. Different species of bees and wasps build different types of nests in various locations, such as eaves, trees, ground cavities, or inside wall voids. Seeing a large number of stinging insects entering or leaving a specific area can indicate the presence of a nest. Attempting to remove a bee or wasp nest yourself can be dangerous. Our bee and wasp removal services prioritize safety and, whenever possible, humane removal or relocation of beneficial species like honeybees. For aggressive species or nests in challenging locations, we safely eliminate the nest and take measures to prevent re-nesting in the future.

Mosquito Management

Mosquitoes are not just annoying; they can also transmit serious diseases like West Nile Virus and Eastern Equine Encephalitis. They breed in standing water and are most active during warmer months, making spending time outdoors unpleasant. Constant buzzing and itchy bites are clear signs of a mosquito problem around your property. Reducing mosquito populations requires identifying and eliminating breeding sources and implementing targeted treatments. Our mosquito control services involve inspecting your property for standing water, treating breeding sites, and applying barrier treatments to reduce mosquito activity in your yard. We can also provide recommendations for ongoing prevention strategies to enjoy your outdoor spaces more comfortably.

Silverfish Control

Silverfish are nocturnal insects that feed on carbohydrates, often found in damp, dark areas like bathrooms, basements, and attics. They are known for their wiggling movement, resembling a fish, and they can damage books, paper, clothing, and other starchy materials. Seeing the silverfish themselves, finding small holes or notches in paper or fabric, or discovering yellowish stains (feces) can indicate a silverfish infestation. Silverfish can be persistent, and their ability to hide makes professional control beneficial. Our silverfish control services focus on treating the harborage areas and reducing moisture, which is essential for their survival. We also provide recommendations to help you eliminate conditions that attract silverfish.

Tick Prevention and Control

Ticks are parasites that can transmit diseases to humans and pets, including Lyme disease and Rocky Mountain spotted fever. They are often found in tall grass, wooded areas, and leaf litter, waiting to attach to a host. Finding ticks on yourself or your pets after spending time outdoors, or experiencing symptoms of tick-borne illnesses, indicates a potential tick problem in your environment. Controlling ticks requires addressing their habitat and implementing preventative measures. Our tick control services involve inspecting your property for tick hotspots, applying targeted treatments to tick-prone areas, and providing recommendations for reducing tick habitats around your yard. Protecting your family and pets from tick-borne diseases is a crucial aspect of our services.

Moth Infestations

Moths can be a nuisance, with some species, like clothes moths and pantry moths, causing damage to clothing, fabrics, and stored food products. Seeing moths flying around indoors (especially small moths) or finding damage to stored items are common signs of a moth infestation. Identifying the type of moth is important as treatment methods vary. Our moth control services involve identifying the species, locating the source of the infestation (e.g., infested food items, contaminated clothing), and implementing targeted treatments and cleaning protocols to eliminate the moths and prevent re-infestation. We also provide advice on proper storage and preventative measures.

Squirrel and Raccoon Removal

While often seen as part of nature, squirrels and raccoons can become significant pests when they enter homes or damage property. They can chew through siding, insulation, and wiring, build nests in attics and chimneys, and create unsanitary conditions. Hearing scratching or scurrying sounds in walls or ceilings, finding damage to the exterior of your home, or seeing the animals entering or exiting your property are signs of a wildlife problem. Removing wildlife requires specialized knowledge and techniques. Our squirrel removal and raccoon removal services are part of our broader wildlife control offerings. We focus on humane removal techniques, followed by exclusion to prevent re-entry, and repair of any damage caused by the animals. Wildlife control is important not only for protecting your property but also for the safety of the animals themselves.
